# LFD Product Vision

## 1. Product Thesis
LFD is a lifting video sharing platform first, and a workout logger second.

Set tracking exists to organize, contextualize, and elevate training videos, not to replace traditional logging apps.

If a user can do one thing extremely well in LFD, it should be this: capture, review, and share a full workout's lifting videos in a seamless vertical viewing flow.

## 2. Core Aim
LFD should become the default destination for training videos, where:
- athletes post and watch serious lifting content,
- coaches and trainers review execution and progress,
- watermarked clips signal credibility and intent,
- workouts are experienced as a narrative video session, not disconnected file uploads.

## 3. Positioning Statement
LFD is to lifting videos what specialized platforms are to short-form media: the place you go first to watch, review, and share real training.

## 4. Brand Meaning
The LFD watermark is not decoration. It is a badge of athletic seriousness.

A clip with LFD branding should communicate:
- this was trained with purpose,
- this is structured and attributable,
- this athlete values standards and progression.

## 5. Product Pillars

### A. Video-First Experience
- Every workout should be watchable as one continuous vertical reel.
- Users should move clip-to-clip with minimal friction (swipe up/down behavior).
- Viewing flow should feel native, fast, and immersive on mobile.

### B. Training Context as Metadata
- Set metadata (exercise, load, reps, RPE, %1RM, location) should enrich video, not dominate UI.
- Data should make clips more useful for coaches and self-review.
- Logging UX should stay lightweight and in service of video creation.

### C. Credible Athlete Identity
- Watermarking and consistent visual treatment create recognizable training identity.
- Profile and feed surfaces should reward quality lifting footage and progression.
- "Serious athlete" should be a product feeling users can sense immediately.

### D. Coach and Community Utility
- Trainers need quick review flows for athlete clips.
- Users should discover notable lifting videos and progression moments.
- Sharing should prioritize high signal (quality footage + context) over generic social noise.

## 6. UX Principles
- Video is the primary content unit.
- The first action after opening a workout should often be "watch" not "edit".
- Controls should be minimal during playback; navigation should rely on natural gestures.
- Any form-heavy interaction must be secondary and compact.
- Mobile experience is the reference standard; desktop is supportive.

## 7. Feature Priorities (Now)
1. Workout video reel as a core interaction on workout and summary surfaces.
2. Reliable upload/replace/delete lifecycle for per-set videos.
3. Strong metadata overlays and watermark consistency.
4. Feed and profile experiences that prioritize playable video sequences.
5. Coach-friendly review patterns (quick scan, drill into clip, compare over time).

## 8. Feature Priorities (Next)
1. Video-first home feed ranking (recent, high-signal lifts, PR moments).
2. Better clip sequencing controls and auto-advance behavior.
3. Coach workflows: saved comments, review queues, athlete follow sets.
4. Distribution hooks (sharing links, external sync where useful).

## 9. Non-Goals (For Now)
- Becoming a generic spreadsheet-style training log.
- Adding heavy analytics at the expense of capture/viewing speed.
- Prioritizing broad social features that dilute training focus.

## 10. Decision Filter
Before shipping any feature, ask:
1. Does this make watching lifting videos faster, better, or more meaningful?
2. Does this strengthen LFD as the default place for training videos?
3. Does this reinforce athlete credibility (watermark + context + quality)?

If the answer is no to most of the above, the feature is likely out of scope.

## 11. Success Metrics
- Workouts with at least one video uploaded.
- Average videos watched per workout session.
- Reel completion rate (users who watch multiple clips in sequence).
- Coach/trainer session depth (clips reviewed per athlete).
- Share and return behavior for watermarked clips.

## 12. Current Direction Alignment (Conversation Summary)
Recent work aligns with this vision:
- Vertical reel-style workout viewing has been introduced.
- Per-set video capture and processing pipeline exists.
- Watermark and metadata overlays are implemented in upload flow.

Next development should keep moving toward a video-native app identity.
